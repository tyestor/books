# Fiction Recommendation Criteria

Source of truth for all fiction recommendations in this repo — fantasy, sci-fi, literature, etc. One genre per conversation. Every recommended book must pass every criterion.

## Criteria

1. No woke books.
2. No overtly left-leaning books. Only select neutral or right-leaning books.
3. Nothing didactic from any political ideology.
4. One series per author. Only select their most appropriate books.
5. Only select books that are of a high quality in all fields.
6. Focus on series of length 3-5 books. Use series that are shorter (including standalones) or longer as fill-ins OR if they are truly exceptional.
7. If I send you any books or franchises, flag books that do not meet any of my criterion.
8. No unfinished series.
9. If a series drops in quality later on, do not recommend it.
10. A series within a larger universe may be evaluated and recommended on its own merits only if it is fully self-contained: its central story and character arcs conclude within it, it requires nothing published before or after it for full understanding, and it reads independently of the universe's other series. If a universe's series are order-dependent — forming one continuous narrative — the entire universe counts as a single series and must pass every criterion as a whole. Only author-defined structures qualify: a recommended series must be a unit the author created, with an internal reading order set by the author. Where an author has divided a universe into discrete, self-contained sub-series, those sub-series may be selected individually and approached in any order. Flat universes — loose standalone books in a shared world or numbered sequence with no author-defined sub-series — are excluded entirely; the recommender must never assemble its own grouping or reading order from them.

## How the criteria are applied

- **1–3:** Screened against the text, not the author's public persona. The test is whether the book foregrounds a contemporary ideological argument — whether it argues at the reader. The mere presence of varied characters is not "message"; sermon structure is. Cuts both directions (e.g., Objectivist sermonizing fails #3 the same way progressive sermonizing does).
- **5:** Judged on consensus craft across prose, plot, character, and worldbuilding — not popularity alone.
- **8:** A series is unfinished if any installment of its narrative arc remains unpublished, including announced future volumes that continue a dangling arc. A complete arc whose universe may receive optional, independent future series still passes.
- **9:** Any consensus decline later in the judged unit disqualifies the whole unit — including a mid-series sag that later recovers.
- **10 (unit determination):** Direct continuations — same protagonists or one continuous story — glue into a single unit with the original, and the whole unit is judged together. Optional, independent series in the same universe do not glue. Only author-created units with an author-set internal order are recommendable; the recommender never assembles its own groupings. When a unit passes every criterion, a complete self-contained arc within it may be recommended alone (sized per #6); the rest is then optional reading, not required.
- **7:** Flags cite the specific criterion failed (e.g., "fails #8 — unfinished since 2011").
